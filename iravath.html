About

Arvin (Autonomous Mobile Robot)

The Iravath is a completition driven project for the ISRO Robotic Competition 2024, was developed within an accelerated timeline of five months (January 2024 – May 2024). Its mechanical structure primarily utilizes FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) technology, employing materials such as PLA and ABS for their exceptional strength, lightweight properties, and manufacturability. Central to the rover’s mobility is the Rocker-Bogie suspension mechanism, which ensures consistent ground contact even on rugged or uneven terrains. This design enables the rover to traverse challenging environments with remarkable stability. To further enhance maneuverability, the rover incorporates a differential gear mechanism, which facilitates balance and seamless navigation through sharp turns and varied terrains. Additionally, its six-wheel steering capability allows for smooth lateral movements, providing exceptional versatility for navigating tight or complex spaces. The Iravath rover integrates autonomous navigation, allowing it to operate both under remote control and independently in dynamic or unknown environments. It is powered by ROS (Robot Operating System) running on a Raspberry Pi, which provides efficient processing and control. An Arduino microcontroller bridges the Raspberry Pi with the motors, ensuring seamless communication and real-time execution. What sets the Iravath apart is its sophisticated autonomous functionality. Equipped with a comprehensive sensor suite—including cameras, LIDAR, and proximity sensors—the rover excels in environmental mapping, obstacle detection, and optimal path planning. These capabilities allow the rover to make real-time decisions based on sensory data, enabling it to perform complex tasks without human intervention. Such autonomy is essential for space exploration and remote operations, where manual control is often infeasible. In conclusion, the Iravath rover represents a cutting-edge fusion of advanced mechanical engineering and autonomous technology. Its robust suspension system, differential drive, and real-time decision-making capabilities position it as a strong contender for the ISRO Robotic Competition and a potential candidate for future space exploration missions.
